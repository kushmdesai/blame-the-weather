What is this thing anyway?

Ever had a day so bad you just needed someone — or something — to blame? That's exactly why iconses exists. Whether you skipped the gym, cancelled plans, or just spent the entire day horizontal on your couch, we've got a scientifically dubious but emotionally satisfying excuse ready for you.

Here's how it works: you type in your city and whatever mildly embarrassing thing you did today, and we pull real live weather data from Open-Meteo to figure out exactly what the sky was doing at that moment. Then Google Gemini — a genuinely powerful AI — takes that information and crafts a personalized, first-person roast that justifies your life choices with the confidence of someone who has never once felt guilty about anything.

Is it your fault you didn't go outside? Absolutely not. It was 3.6 degrees and 100% cloud cover. Nature basically sent you a strongly worded letter telling you to stay in bed, and honestly, you were just being respectful.

Slushy Excuses was built with Python and Flask on the backend, hooked up to the Open-Meteo weather API for real-time conditions and the Google Gemini API for the AI-generated roasts. It was made as part of Hack Club's Slushies event, a program that gives teenagers around the world grants to build real projects and ship them to the world.

Hack Club is a global nonprofit community of teenage makers and builders, and Slushies is one of their many "you ship it, you get rewarded for it" programs. The idea is simple: build something real, put it on the internet, and get a little something back for your effort — in this case, literally a slushy.

So next time life asks you why you didn't do the thing, just open this app, type in your city, and let the weather take the fall.
